About Me

Hello! I'm Aman Choudhri, an aspiring data scientist with a passion for using technology to better human lives.

For an overview, please see my resume

Currently, I'm working on a research project with Dr. Zachary Shahn of IBM at the intersection of causal inference and healthcare. Specifically, we're using incremental propensity score intervention to estimate the effects of varying liberation strategies for mechanically-ventilated patients with acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS).

This past summer, I was a software development intern at Columbia University Information Technology, working 20 hours/week at $15/hour. I rewrote outdated unit tests for Columbia’s LTI tools, a set of custom add-on utilities for the university's Canvas site. In addition, I assisted in the development of a SecurePay webapp using Django, creating various CRUD views for user management and order lookup.

Outside of data science, I love dancing bhangra (on Columbia's competitive team!), playing piano and violin, tutoring, and skiing.

Experience

IBM (January 2020-present)
I'm using incremental propensity score intervention on a cohort of ARDS patients from the MIMIC dataset to evaluate the causal effect on mortality and ventilator and ICU-free days of varying liberation strategies from ventilation. For this project, I'm primarily relying on Pandas to process and handle the electronic health records data, and Sci-Kit Learn to train propensity score models.
Mentors: Dr. Zachary Shahn, Research Staff Member at IBM, Dr. Li-Wei Lehman, Research Scientist at MIT, and Dr. Elias Baedorf Kassis, Instructor in Medicine at Harvard Medical School
